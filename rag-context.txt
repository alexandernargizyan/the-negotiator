The Negotiator is an ElevenLabs voice agent for the 5th Global AI Hack-Nation Hackathon. It calls moving companies, compares quotes, and negotiates prices. The judge plays the role of a moving company sales representative with 3 different personalities.

## 2. MOVING PRICE BENCHMARKS (FMCSA Data 2025-2026)

### Local Moves (same state, under 100 miles)
- Studio/1 bedroom: $500 - $1,200
- 2 bedroom: $1,000 - $2,200
- 3 bedroom: $1,500 - $3,200
- 4+ bedroom: $2,200 - $4,500

### Long Distance Moves (over 100 miles)
- Studio: $1,200 - $2,500
- 1 bedroom: $1,800 - $3,500
- 2 bedroom: $2,500 - $5,500
- 3 bedroom: $3,500 - $7,500
- 4+ bedroom: $5,000 - $11,000

### Rock Hill, SC → Charlotte, NC (30 miles, local)
- 2 bedroom: $1,200 - $2,500
- Estimated fair price: $1,700 - $2,200 all-inclusive

### Additional Fees
- Packing service: $200 - $800 per room
- Stairs fee (per flight): $75 - $250
- Elevator fee: $0 - $100 (often free)
- Long carry (over 75 ft): $50 - $200 per 100 ft
- Fuel surcharge: 10-15% of base
- Basic insurance/valuation: $50 - $200
- Peak season (May-September): +15-25%
- Shuttle service (if truck can't fit): $200 - $500
- Storage: $100 - $300 per month
- Assembly/disassembly: $50 - $150 per item

## 3. SALES REP STYLES (for demo)

### Style A: Hard Negotiator
Personality: Busy, no-nonsense, curt but professional.
Pricing: High base price, aggressive add-on fees.
Typical answers:
- "Base is $1,800. Stairs $400 extra. Packing $300."
- "Those are our prices. Take it or leave it."
- "I can't do any better than that."
- "We have a minimum charge of $2,000."
- "You want insurance? That's another $150."

### Style B: Lowballer with Hidden Fees
Personality: Friendly, eager to please, sounds cheap.
Pricing: Very low base price, huge add-on fees.
Typical answers:
- "Only $1,100 base! Great deal!"
- "Packing? That's $400 extra. Stairs? $350."
- "Oh, you need an elevator? Actually we charge $200 for that."
- "The base is low because we have to add the fuel surcharge separately."

### Style C: Straight Shooter
Personality: Professional, transparent, helpful.
Pricing: Fair base price, clear itemized fees.
Typical answers:
- "Here's the itemized quote: $1,600 base, $200 stairs, $100 fuel."
- "If you can book by end of week, I can take $100 off."
- "I'd recommend getting full value protection — it's only $80 extra."
- "Honestly, for a move like yours, $1,800 all-inclusive is fair."

## 4. COMMON PUSHBACKS & HOW THE AGENT HANDLES THEM

### "We don't give quotes over the phone — we need to see the place."
Agent: "I understand. Could you give me a rough estimate based on the details I provided? A ballpark range is fine. My customer wants to compare before committing to an in-person visit."

### "Someone will call you back."
Agent: "I appreciate that. Could you give me a preliminary price range right now? Even approximate. I'd like to give my customer some options today."

### "What's your budget?"
Agent: "I'd prefer to hear your price first. We're comparing multiple companies to find the best value."

### "Can I speak to the customer directly?"
Agent: "I'm authorized to handle this on their behalf. All decisions go through me."

### "We don't negotiate."
Agent: "I understand. Thank you for the quote. I'll include it in my comparison."

### "That's our final price."
Agent: "I see. I have a competing quote for $1,850 from another company. If you can match or beat that, I'd be happy to recommend you."

## 5. PRICE RED FLAGS & WARNINGS

- Price 30%+ below average → likely lowball with hidden fees (ask: "Can you confirm this is all-inclusive?")
- Price 50%+ above average → overcharging (ask: "Can you itemize what makes this higher?")
- No written estimate → FMCSA requires one for interstate moves
- No USDOT number → unregistered carrier, illegal
- Large deposit requested (over 20%) → potential scam
- "We'll refund any difference" → vague promise
- Cash-only payment → red flag

---

## 6. FMCSA REGULATIONS

- All interstate movers need a USDOT number
- Must provide written estimate before moving
- Must provide "Your Rights and Responsibilities When You Move" booklet
- Must offer arbitration program
- Non-binding estimate: final price can't exceed 110% without customer approval
- Binding estimate: guaranteed price, can't be changed
- Customers have 9 months to file a complaint

## 8. MOVING FAQ

Q: What affects moving cost the most?
A: Distance, number of rooms, weight, stairs, packing services, and season.

Q: How far in advance should I book movers?
A: 4-8 weeks for peak season (May-Sept), 2-4 weeks for off-peak.

Q: Is moving insurance worth it?
A: Basic coverage is free (60¢/lb). Full value protection costs more but covers full replacement.

Q: Should I tip movers?
A: $20-$40 per mover per day is standard, more for exceptional service.

Q: What's the cheapest time to move?
A: Mid-month, mid-week (Tue-Wed), October-April.

## 9. NEGOTIATION TACTICS

### Tactics the Agent Uses:
1. **Competing leverage**: "I have a quote for $1,850 — can you beat it?"
2. **Itemization pressure**: "Can you break that down so I can compare apples to apples?"
3. **Future business**: "If this goes well, I have other customers with similar moves."
4. **Timing**: "If I book today, can you do better?"
5. **Package bundling**: "Can you waive the stairs fee if I also book packing?"

### Tactics the Agent Should Expect from Sales Reps:
1. **Upselling**: "You'll definitely need premium packing for that china cabinet."
2. **Urgency**: "That price is only good if you book by end of day."
3. **Confusion**: Multiple different fee categories to hide the true total.
4. **Deflection**: "Let me transfer you to our pricing specialist."
5. **Bait-and-switch**: Low base + surprise additional fees.

## 10. EXAMPLE CONVERSATION FLOW

Agent: "I have a customer moving from Rock Hill, SC to Charlotte, NC. 2-bedroom, stairs at destination, August 1st. Can you give me a quote?"
